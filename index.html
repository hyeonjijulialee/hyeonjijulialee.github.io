Hello!

I am Hyeonji (usually go by Julia) Lee, an undergraduate student majoring in Computer Science at Washington State University. I am applying to graduate programs in Information Schools (iSchools) for Fall 2026.

I believe that the volume, accuracy, accessibility, and practicality of information are closely tied to individual autonomy and quality of life. However, socioeconomic inequality, technology accessibility gaps, and differences in information literacy continue to create significant disparities.

My goal is to design systems and educational approaches that enable people to access information equitably, interpret it effectively, and apply it meaningfully in their daily lives. Through this work, I hope to reduce information disparities and ultimately improve people’s quality of life.

Through my PhD studies, I seek to advance the field of information science through rigorous research and education, developing innovative solutions that can bring substantial and positive changes to society.

Thanks again for visiting! If you have any questions, feel free to reach out through the contact information on the side.

Research Interests

I explore how people access, interpret, and utilize information, with the goal of designing equitable and user-centered systems and educational approaches.
