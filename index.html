Research Interests

Human Information Behavior · AI in Information Literacy Education · Information Retrieval Systems · User-Centered Systems and Services

Hello, and thank you for visiting my website!
I'm Hyeonji Lee (I usually go by Julia), an undergraduate student majoring in Computer Science at Washington State University. I'm currently preparing to apply to iSchool graduate programs in Library and Information Science for Fall 2026.
I believe that information volume, accuracy, accessibility, and practicality are directly correlated with individual autonomy and quality of life. However, various factors, including socioeconomic inequality, technology accessibility gaps, and differences in information literacy, create significant information disparities in our society.
I want to contribute to bridging these gaps by leveraging my technical expertise and research capabilities. My goal is to research and design systems and educational approaches that help people access information equitably, interpret it effectively, and utilize it meaningfully in their daily lives. Through this work, I hope to reduce information disparities and ultimately improve people's overall quality of life.
Through my PhD studies, I seek to advance the field of information science through rigorous research and education, developing innovative solutions that can bring substantial and positive changes to society.
Thanks again for visiting! If you have any questions, feel free to reach out through the contact information above.
