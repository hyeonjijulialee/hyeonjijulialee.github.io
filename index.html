Hello, and thank you for visiting my website!
I'm Hyeonji Lee (I usually go by Julia), an undergraduate student majoring in Computer Science at Washington State University. I'm currently preparing to apply to iSchool graduate programs in Library and Information Science for Fall 2026.
As a research assistant to Professor Tingting Li, I contributed to a conference proposal paper exploring the use of AI in analyzing concept maps and educational data. I also work on the Kimble Project at WSU Libraries’ Manuscripts, Archives, and Special Collections (MASC), where I support digitization, metadata creation, and front desk services. On campus, I’m actively involved in student communities such as the Society of Women Engineers (SWE), the Students Book Corporation (SBC), the Voiland Peer Network Mentorship Program, and the Team Mentoring Program (TMP), where I support peer engagement and student success.
My long-term goal is to contribute to the advancement of information science through research and education, and one day to fulfill a personal dream of co-authoring a Library and Information Science paper with both of my parents.
Outside of academics, I enjoy traveling, exploring national parks, hiking, photography, and singing. Lately, I've been trying to read more regularly as a way to build a lasting habit and focus on personal growth. At home, I live with a clever two-year-old cat who knows how to sit, shake, high-five, play dead, and touch his nose to a ring.
